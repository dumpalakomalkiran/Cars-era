Mahindra Verito Review

Summary

Mahindra had brought the Renault Logan to India with an intent to venture in to the sedan space from their bastion - the SUVs. After parting ways with Renault, Mahindra continued the car with a new name - the Mahindra Verito. The car received significant updates in 2012 making it more stylish and contemporary. Mahindra is gone one step ahead now, bringing the fully electric version of the sedan to the production market - a first in its class.

Further to this, read Mahindra Verito Road Test details by our expert.

Exterior

With all new front and rear styling, the new model looks more refreshing and outgoing than the old one. It gets a new sculpted front bumper, a reworked hood bonnet, new fog lamps and a front grille with loads of chrome. As you move towards the sides, the profile is the same with the flared wheel arches and a new pair of alloys. The rear design, too, is completely changed with new clear lens tail lamps, new chrome applique, new deck lid and bumper. The new alloys and blackened pillars are offered with the top-end variant only.

Interior

The interior of the car is now more premium and feature-rich than before. It gets a new 2-DIN music system with CD/ MP3/ Tuner, USB and Aux-in connectivity, new upholstery, dual tone instrument panel, modified centre console along with contemporary grey interior theme. The interior ergonomics is much improved with revised grab handles, door mounted power window switches, electric ORVM control ad new AC controls layout. The sedan is quite spacious and equipped with lots of goodies.

Engine Transmission

Powering the saloon is the same1461cc, 1.5-litre dCi CRDi diesel engine that churns out a maximum power output of 65bhp at 4000rpm with a peak torque of 160Nm from 2000rpm. The oil burner is mated to a 5-speed manual transmission and sends power to the front wheels. The e-Verito is powered by 41bhp(30.5kW) motor that is capable of pushing the sedan to a top speed of 86kmph. If you are not driving it fast, you can expect to harness the maximum out of the 100km range of the Verito. For short spins, you can quick charge the e-Verito in one hour 45 minutes to 80 per cent but the full range opens up only when you give it a full charge of eight hours 45 minutes.

Performance & Handling

Engine performance and city drivability are pretty impressive. It has minimal turbo-lag and the in-gear acceleration is good too. It’s easy to drive, especially at low revs. The clutch is extremely light, and the gear shift ratio is short. The steering wheel doesn’t weigh up at low and high speeds. NVH levels are well-controlled. However, the body roll is an issue.

Braking System

The braking capabilities are good. It gets disc brakes up front and drum brakes in the rear, while ABS (Anti-lock braking system) with EBD (Electronic brake-force distribution) are offered on the top-variant only.

Mileage

The 1461 cc Diesel - powered variant delivers a fuel economy of 19 kmpl in city and 21 kmpl on highways, while the Electric variant offers a mileage of 100 kmpl on urban roads and 102 kmpl and freeways, respectively.

Safety Features

The driver airbag, anti-lock braking system (ABS) with electronic brake-force distribution (EBD) and electrical rear window child lock are available only on top-end model.

Verdict

With all new looks, premium interior and better ergonomics, the new Verito makes a great choice for buyers. It’s one of the most fuel efficient and fun-to-drive Mahindra cars in India.
