Ford EcoSport Review

Summary

Launched in 2013, Ford Ecosport got an update in 2015. This includes a new LED strip in the headlamps, more features on offer like automatic headlamps, rain sensing wipers and at the same time the diesel engine has seen a bump in power of 8bhp. The 1.5-litre DV5 engine now has a power output of 98bhp and a maximum torque of 215Nm. The Ford Ecosport is one of the most feature loaded compact SUV in its segment. This compact SUV is a five-seater SUV that is under four metres in length.

The Ford Ecosport is available with two petrol and one diesel engine options. This new compact SUV is the first in its segment and now Maruti, Tata and even Hyundai are working on products to get into this segment. The Ecosport comes loaded with a lot of features for convenience and even safety aspects as well.

Further to this, read Ford EcoSport Road Test details by our expert.

Exteriors

The Ford Ecosport is based on the Kinetic Design theme. The sleek front grille with the hexagonal lower grille looks stylish and the slender headlamps add on to the styling. The Ecosport’s headlamp now even has integrated LEDs that work as daytime running lamps.The fog lamps have chrome surrounds.  The side profile of the Ford Ecosport looks sporty with the small glass area and the larger metal area. The alloy wheels look a bit tiny and the attempt to make it look muscular gets missed out somewhere. The tailgate of the Ecosport slides open and it doesn’t pop like a hatch. The spare wheel is mounted on the tail gate. The Ford Ecosport is available in eight colours, namely, Chill Metallic, Diamond White, Golden Bronze, Kinetic Blue, Mars Red, Moondust Silver, Panther Black, Smoke Grey.

Interiors

The Ecosport has modern interiors with its centre console looking almost like a cockpit. The all-new interiors of the Ecosport makes it look trendy. There are too many buttons on the centre console, which could get a bit confusing. The blue backlight interiors on the Ecosport even have ambient lighting. The twin dial instrument cluster is easy to read and there is a basic driver information system too. The three-spoke Ford steering wheel with controls for music system and telephone are there on the left hand side. The Ford Ecosport has reverse parking sensors and even climate control air-con on the top variant, Titanium Plus.

The space at the front and even the rear is good. The thigh support is decent. The rear seats recline and this makes it a lot more comfortable. The Ecosport has good space at the rear.

Engine and Transmission

The Ecosport is offered with three engine options. One is 1.0-litre Ecoboost that churns about 120bhp of power and is a very small and light engine. This engine has a torque of 170Nm at 1400-4500rpm.The second engine on offer is the 1.5-litre petrol and this produces about 110bhp of power and comes mated to a five-speed manual or a six-speed dual clutch transmission. The third engine on offer is the 1.5-litre diesel that churns about 98bhp of power and 215Nm of peak torque. This engine comes mated to a five-speed manual transmission. The diesel engine has been updated for more power as it now has been bumped up by 8bhp.

Performance and Handling

Fords are known for their driving dynamics and the Ecosport is no different. This compact SUV has one of the best handling characteristics. The Ecosport’s Ecoboost engine is the performance one if you need something that has good performance. The Ecosport’s diesel engine has decent performance. 

Braking System

The Ford Ecosport has one of the best braking systems in the segment. It has front and rear disc brakes as a standard feature. This isn’t common on other compact SUVs. There is ABS with EBD, traction control and even ESP on the petrol automatic variant.

Mileage

The 1498 cc Diesel - powered variant delivers a fuel economy of 20 kmpl in city and 22 kmpl on highways, while the Petrol variant offers a mileage of 16 kmpl on urban roads and 18 kmpl and freeways, respectively.

Safety Features

Ford has ensured that the Ecosport comes loaded with the best in class safety features. It has six airbags on the top variant, anti-local braking system with EBD, traction control system on petrol automatic, hill assist and reverse parking sensors. All the passengers get seat belt as well.

Verdict

The Ford Ecosport is a great compact SUV to consider. It is stylish, feature-loaded and has an array on engines and transmissions on offer. If you want to make a statement, the Ford Ecosport is the SUV you need to get for yourself.

Competition

The Ford Ecosport competes with the Mahindra TUV300 and the Maruti Suzuki Vitara Brezza. These are the new vehicles that are competing in the compact SUV segment with the Vitara Brezza being the latest entrant.
