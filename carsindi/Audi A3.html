Audi A3 Review

Summary

Audi has been a highly successful luxury car brand in India and with the Q3 adding a majority numbers, the A3 was the next to enter. Audi won the World Car of the Year award for the A3 and this compact sedan made its way into India in 2014. The A3 is the twelfth product from the Ingolstadt-based automaker in India. This is the smallest sedan and the newest sedan member to the family of A4, A6, A7 and A8.

The Audi A3 is the most affordable product to the Audi brand. With this compact sedan, Audi’s entry level price reduced. while the other German competition are introducing premium hatchbacks, Audi has opted not to go the hatchback route for the Indian luxury car market. The Audi A3 is based on the MQB platform, which it shares with the Volkswagen Jetta and the Skoda Octavia.

Further to this, read Audi A3 Road Test details by our expert.

Exterior

The Audi A3 might be mistaken for an A4 at times, however it is a lot different when viewed closely. This is the compact sedan and it has lesser chrome than that on the A4. The front grille is smaller and the headlamps too are smaller in size. The higher variants of the A3 get daytime running LEDs, while the lower variants have a bulb.

The side profile and even the fascia is very Audi-ish and this makes the A3 resemble the A4 in many ways. The design is simple with crisp lines running across the vehicle. The A3 has subtle usage of chrome all across the body. It is the rear where the A3 stands out from the other Audis. It has different style for the tail lamps that makes it differentiate from the existing family.

Interior

The Audi has a new interior design for the A3, which is its smallest and most affordable product in India. The A3 has a black and beige interior that looks stylish and it has several new elements. The centre screen is a pop-up and the music system is the MMI generation two, which simplifies usage even more. The AC vents are turbine in shape and this makes it easier to redirect air as diffused or in concentrated form. The instrument cluster is somewhat different and the other good point of the A3 is the ergonomics. The centre console has been considerably changed in the new A3. Instead, the rotary knob is the single operating command centre. Technology has been improved by introducing a touch pad on the rotary knob, which is capable of identifying handwriting with accuracy.

There is sufficient space in the front row for tall people, while the space in the rear seat is a bit too tight. The boot on the A3 is large enough to carry luggage for a weekend.

Engine and Transmission

Audi is offering the A3 with a 1.8-litre TFSI engine, which is a turbo petrol engine that comes mated to a seven-speed dual clutch transmission. It produces 177bhp of power and 250Nm of peak torque. The second engine is a 2.0-litre diesel that churns 142bhp of power and about 320Nm of max torque. This engine comes mated to a six-speed DSG. The Audi A3 is a front-wheel drive compact sedan.

Performance and Handling

Audi have been fun to drive and the A3 is no different. The smallest Audi in India behaves and performs like any other Audi does. This makes it a highly favourable compact sedan. The petrol and diesel both have good enough performance to overtake and joy twisties. The dual clutch transmission ensures smooth and quick shifts, thus enhancing the response time. The diesel is highly frugal too, and this can be a great mile muncher.

Braking System

The A3 comes with disc brakes for all four wheels. This means that the braking is better and with the ABS, it is far more controlled too. The A3 gets ESP and even traction control system as well.

Mileage

The 1968 cc Diesel - powered variant delivers a fuel economy of 16 kmpl in city and 18 kmpl on highways, while the Petrol variant offers a mileage of 16 kmpl on urban roads and 18 kmpl and freeways, respectively.

Safety Features

Audi has ensured that the A3 gets dual airbags and ABS as a standard feature. This compact sedan even gets hill hold control, brake assistance and ESP as well. The parking brake is electronic on the A3.

Verdict

The Audi A3 offers great styling, features and even the price at which it is offered. This makes it a good offering, however the rear seat knee room is a bit tight on this compact sedan from Audi.

Competition

As competition, the Mercedes-Benz CLA is the only rival to the Audi A3. Both offer different USPs. BMW 1 Series Sedan will be another new product to look out in this segment, but there is still sometime for it to be launched.
